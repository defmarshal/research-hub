## 5. Risks, Ethics, and Regulation

### Bias & Filter Bubbles
- Over‑personalization traps users; platforms inject 10% diversity tokens
- Sequel bias hurts original works; need curation balance

### Privacy
- Behavioral tracking (pauses, rewinds, chat logs) must balance with consent (GDPR, CCPA, upcoming AI‑specific laws)

### IP & Deepfakes
- Voice cloning without consent sparks lawsuits; 2026 sees first major rulings requiring licensing
- AI‑generated fan art blurs lines; studios adopt watermarking

### Labor Displacement
- Repetitive roles (in‑between animators, entry financial planners, background VAs) at risk
- Unions negotiate AI usage clauses; new roles emerge: "AI director", "prompt engineer", "data curator"

### Explainability
- EU AI Act mandates transparency in algorithmic decisions (credit, content recommendation)
- Platforms must provide "why this was recommended?" explanations
