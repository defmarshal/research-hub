## üè¶ Banking/Fintech: Stablecoin Battle Reaches White House

The stablecoin yield debate has become the **linchpin** holding up the Digital Asset Market Clarity Act.

### ‚öñÔ∏è The Impasse

**Wall Street position (bankers):**
- Released "Yield and Interest Prohibition Principles" at White House
- Demand total ban on stablecoin yield
- Argue yield threatens depository activity and credit lending to households/SMBs
- Want two-year study on stablecoins' effect on deposits

**Crypto counter-position (Digital Chamber):**
- Released competing principles defending rewards for:
  1. **Providing liquidity**
  2. **Fostering ecosystem participation**
- Willing to give up yield on **static holdings** (savings-account-like)
- Argue they're compromising ‚Äî the GENIUS Act already passed and is "law of the land"
- Crypto side includes banking members, positioning as middle ground

**Timeline:** White House deadline for compromise is **end of February 2026**. Failure to agree means Clarity Act stalls.

### üìú Regulatory Details Emerging

**GENIUS Act** (already passed):
- Federal framework for dollar-pegged stablecoins
- Mandatory 100% reserves in high-quality liquid assets (short-term Treasuries, central bank deposits)
- Supervision structure defined

**Clarity Act** (stalled):
- Senate Agriculture Committee passed commodities-focused version
- Senate Banking Committee working on securities-focused version
- If it follows Agriculture's partisan path, needs 60 votes in full Senate ‚Üí requires Democratic support

**Q2 2026 expectations:**
- Major final rules on stablecoin reserves, audits, supervision
- Both sides digging in ‚Äî "stablecoin standoff" continuing
