## Executive Summary

The global anime industry reached a record **3.84 trillion yen ($25.1 billion)** in 2024, growing 14.8% year-on-year. For the third consecutive year, overseas markets surpassed domestic revenue, with international sales reaching **2.17 trillion yen ($14.1 billion)**—a staggering 26% increase—accounting for 56% of total revenue. Meanwhile, the domestic Japanese market showed minimal growth of just 2.8%, raising concerns about market saturation and stagnation. The production side of the industry grew 9.1% to 466.2 billion yen ($3.03 billion), with overseas production revenue up 16.6%.

## 2. Key Growth Drivers

### 2.1 Global Streaming Platforms

The explosive growth since 2020 is directly attributed to worldwide streaming distribution:

- **Platform Expansion:** Netflix, Disney+, Crunchyroll, and others have accelerated anime accessibility
- **COVID-19 Impact:** Pandemic lockdowns drove digital consumption; legacy effects continue
- **Licensing Revenue:** Major income source, though primarily from licensing deals rather than direct involvement in international merchandising/events

> "Overseas revenue is now climbing high and has not yet reached the peak. We believe it has potential to expand to even more markets." — Megumi Onouchi, AJA committee member

### 2.2 Merchandising & Events

- **Overseas Events:** 160 anime-related events across 50 countries (numbers increasing annually)
- **Merchandising:** Strong domestic performer (4.8B USD) but untapped potential internationally
- **Music & Concerts:** Growing segment within broader market

### 2.3 IP Strength & Gaming Synergy

- **Japanese IP Advantage:** Anime, manga, and gaming franchises show extremely high growth potential
- **Cross-media Success:** Characters like Gundam, Godzilla, One Piece demonstrate Hollywood viability
- **Sony/Nintendo Ecosystem:** Gaming brands amplify anime IP globally

## 3. Critical Challenges

### 3.1 Domestic Market Stagnation

The domestic Japanese anime market shows alarming signs of saturation:

- **Minimal Growth:** Only 2.8% increase in 2024, with inflation-adjusted analysis suggesting flat or shrinking real market
- **Stagnant Revenue Sources:** Television program sales to stations have plateaued
- **Catalog Exhaustion:** While new content acquisitions remain robust, older catalog titles procurement has largely run its course
- **Consumer Trends:** Inflation in culture/recreation at 5.4% (2024) outpaces revenue growth

### 3.2 Production Capacity Constraints

The production side faces severe structural challenges:

- **Labor Shortage:** Chronic personnel shortage; domestic production capacity nearing limits
- **Revenue Concentration:** Growth increasingly concentrated in larger studios; smaller studios struggling
- **Time Lag:** Overseas streaming revenue takes years to reflect in production-side revenues

> "The domestic anime production scene faces a chronic shortage of personnel, and domestic production capacity is already nearing its limits." — Anime journalist Atsushi Matsumoto

### 3.3 Geographic Expansion Pressures

To address capacity, major studios are expanding internationally:

**Toei Animation's Ambitious Plan:**
- Establishing new studios in Southeast Asia by 2030
- Manila studio (since 1992, fully owned since 1999) already handles 70% of production workload
- Investment: 24B yen ($156M) for new studios + 70B yen ($455M) in IP production + 39B yen ($254M) in international licensing
- Goal: 50% increase in production lines; long-term target of 500B yen ($3.25B) revenue beyond 2030

## 5. Emerging Trends & Future Outlook

### 5.1 Content Trends

- **Sequels & Remakes:** Industry research predicts surge in sequel productions and remakes of 90s/00s hits
- **Global Appeal vs. Authenticity:** Debate over balancing international appeal with Japanese cultural specificity
- **Diverse Genres:** Continued expansion beyond traditional shonen/seinen into josei, boys' love, and experimental formats

- **Digital Workflows:** Investment in digital production processes (Toei's 24B yen allocation)

### 5.3 Talent Development

**CloverWorks' Flint Base Initiative:**
- New division to recruit/train graduating students as in-between animators
- 2-year training program to address labor shortages

## 6. Implications & Strategic Considerations

### For Japanese Studios:

1. **Double Down on International Expansion:** Overseas markets still far from saturation; focus on direct merchandising/events, not just licensing
2. **Diversify Production Geographically:** Southeast Asia investments critical to scale capacity
3. **Domestic Market Innovation:** Need to reinvigorate domestic consumption beyond traditional TV/film models

### For International Investors/Companies:

1. **Licensing vs Co-Production:** Consider deeper partnerships beyond pure licensing (shared IP development)
2. **Localized Merchandising:** Gap in international merchandise distribution presents opportunity
3. **Event-Based Revenue:** Anime conventions and cultural events growing globally

### For Content Creators:

1. **Global Mindset:** Design properties with international appeal from inception (e.g., Disney Twisted-Wonderland model)
2. **Multi-Platform Strategy:** Anime + gaming + merch ecosystem essential for modern IP

## 8. Conclusion

The anime industry stands at a pivotal moment: unprecedented global demand is driving record revenues, but domestic stagnation and production bottlenecks threaten long-term sustainability. The successful players will be those who:

- **Embrace global markets** with direct operations, not just licensing
- **Invest in capacity** through geographic diversification and technology
- **Innovate domestically** to re-engage Japanese audiences
- **Balance authenticity** with international appeal

The next 5-10 years will determine whether the "New Cool Japan Strategy" can achieve its 20 trillion yen ambition and whether the industry can evolve from a licensing powerhouse to a truly global entertainment conglomerate.
