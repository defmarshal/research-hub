# Banking & Fintech Developments - February 2026
**Date:** 2026-02-19  
**Topic:** Banking, Fintech, CBDC, Cryptocurrency  
**Sources:** Central Banking, CoinLaw, JM Financial Services, HKMA

## Executive Summary

The banking and fintech landscape in early 2026 is characterized by accelerating regulatory evolution, the rise of stablecoin legitimacy through federal charters, and ongoing tensions between traditional banking institutions and crypto-native companies. Central Bank Digital Currency (CBDC) development continues globally, though most central banks still lack clear legal mandates. The United States is seeing a significant shift as crypto firms like Stripe's Bridge secure conditional OCC approval for national trust bank charters, potentially reshaping the regulatory framework for stablecoin operations.

## Major Developments

### 1. CBDC Mandate Gap Persists

**Key Finding:** According to Central Banking's Fintech Benchmarks 2026, 71.4% of central banks (28 respondents) lack legal powers to issue CBDCs. This represents a slight improvement from 69% in 2025 but indicates most jurisdictions still lack clear regulatory frameworks.

**Context:** The benchmark surveyed central banks with median workforce of 891 employees (average 1,483), providing insights into global central bank readiness.

**Implications:**
- CBDC development remains in research/pilot phases rather than implementation for most countries
- Legal uncertainty continues to slow adoption
- Hong Kong Monetary Authority (HKMA) continues active research since 2017 for e-HKD as "complement to existing forms of money" (last updated Feb 16, 2026)
- Politicization: Approximately 20% of jurisdictions report CBDCs becoming divisive political issues

### 2. Stripe's Bridge Secures OCC Conditional Approval (Major Breakthrough)

**Event:** February 17, 2026 - Bridge (Stripe-owned stablecoin platform) received conditional OCC approval to establish a **national trust bank**.

**Significance:** This represents a watershed moment for stablecoin integration into traditional banking infrastructure. Bridge will operate under direct federal oversight for:
- **Custody** of digital assets for business customers
- **Stablecoin issuance** and orchestration
- **Reserves management**
- **Stablecoin orchestration**

**Strategic Benefits:**
- Single federal compliance lane reduces need for state-level money transmitter licenses
- Positions Bridge to serve "enterprises, fintechs, crypto businesses and financial institutions" with "stablecoin products and services inside a clear federal framework"
- Integration with Stripe's Open Issuance platform already supports Phantom's CASH and MetaMask's mUSD

**Competitive Context:**
- Bridge joins Ripple, Circle, Paxos, Fidelity Digital Assets, BitGo (all with conditional approvals Dec 2025)
- Erebor Bank granted conditional national bank charter in October 2025
- Timeline for final approval not announced by OCC

**Industry Reaction:**
- American Bankers Association (ABA) pushing back, arguing GENIUS Act provisions remain unclear
- Bankers担心 stablecoin companies could "sidestep oversight" through national trust charters
- Tension between crypto firms seeking national scale and banks demanding consistent standards

### 3. Stablecoin Regulatory Framework - The GENIUS Act

**Legislative Context:** The Guiding and Establishing National Innovation for U.S. Stablecoins (GENIUS) Act passed in 2025, establishing regulatory framework for stablecoin issuers.

**Current Status:** Federal regulators (OCC, Federal Reserve, FDIC) have **not yet instituted specific regulations** mandated by the Act, though process is underway.

**Bridge's Position:** Bridge states systems already meet GENIUS Act compliance standards, positioning for final approval.

**Market Impact:** Companies positioning early for rulebook still being written - indicates strong anticipated demand.

### 4. China's CBDC Expansion

**Developments:** China continues advancing e-CNY:
- **Salary disbursements**纳入 CBDC trials
- **Cross-border trade settlements** being tested
- Strategic intent to "replace decentralized crypto with state-sanctioned digital money"
- Enforcement surge in crypto regulations to control financial system

**Philosophy:** State-controlled digital currency as alternative to decentralized cryptocurrencies.

### 5. Hong Kong's e-HKD Research

**Status:** HKMA actively advancing e-HKD research since 2017.
- Established CBDC Expert Group with academic leaders from Chinese University of Hong Kong, HKUST, University of Hong Kong, etc.
- Last updated expert group membership February 16, 2026
- Approach: complement existing money forms rather than replace

## Regulatory Landscape

### Federal vs. State Tension

**Executive Order:** President Trump signed December 2025 order designed to preempt state-level AI regulations - framing patchwork of 50 laws would "strangle innovation" and advantage China.

**Counter-concern:** Without robust federal regulation, consumer protections may be inadequate.

**Lobbying:** OpenAI and Meta reportedly deploying significant resources to influence AI/fintech regulation debate.

### EU's Compliance Pressure

While EU AI Act focuses on AI, implications for fintech AI applications are significant:
- High-risk AI applications (credit scoring, hiring, educational assessment) face strict requirements
- Fines up to 4% of global annual revenue
- Non-EU businesses serving European customers subject regardless of incorporation location

### U.S. State vs. Federal AI Regulation Battle

Parallel to fintech, AI regulation seeing intense federal-state conflict:
- Federal executive order vs. state-level laws
- AI companies heavily lobbying both sides
- Creates compliance uncertainty - businesses advised to "build for stricter standard"

## Challenges and Risks

### 1. Regulatory Uncertainty

- Most central banks lack CBDC issuance mandate (71.4%)
- Federal regulations for stablecoins still pending despite conditional approvals
- State vs. federal conflicts creating compliance complexity
- Cross-border harmonization lacking

### 2. Traditional Banking Pushback

- ABA and banking groups concern about "moving too fast"
- Fear of crypto firms sidestepping oversight via trust charters
- Competitive threat to traditional payment and deposit services
- Potential destabilization if stablecoins gain significant market share without equivalent prudential requirements

### 3. Energy Considerations

Similar to AI data centers, blockchain/crypto infrastructure facing energy scrutiny. ESG considerations becoming material for crypto operations.

### 4. Security and Resilience

**AI Agent Security:** Every AI agent needs same security protections as human employees - access control, audit trails, identity verification. Prompt injection attacks demonstrated in 2025.

**Quantum Computing Timeline:** IBM declares 2026 "year of quantum advantage" - first time quantum computers will outperform classical on meaningful problems. Long-term threat to current cryptographic protocols used in blockchain.
